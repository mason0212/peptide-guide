SS-31

What It Is & How It Works

SS-31 (Elamipretide) is a synthetic tetrapeptide (D-Arg-2′6′-dimethylTyr-Lys-Phe-NH2) that targets mitochondria, binding to cardiolipin to stabilize the inner mitochondrial membrane. This enhances the electron transport chain, boosts ATP production, and reduces reactive oxygen species (ROS), mitigating oxidative stress. It crosses the blood-brain barrier, concentrating in mitochondria for systemic effects.

Benefits

  • Energy Enhancement: Improves ATP synthesis, crucial for high-energy tissues like muscles and neurons, enhancing cellular energy by optimizing mitochondrial bioenergetics.
  • Anti-Aging: Delays cellular aging by protecting mitochondria from oxidative damage, potentially improving skin health, vitality, and longevity.
  • Neuroprotection: Reduces brain oxidative stress, supporting cognitive function in neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s.
  • Cardioprotection: Enhances heart function by reducing ischemia/reperfusion injury, improving cardiac energy metabolism.

Clinical Studies

Szeto et al. (2014) showed reduced ischemia/reperfusion injury (PubMed: 24801158); Phase II trials (2020) improved cardiac function (ClinicalTrials.gov: NCT02814097); randomized trials for mitochondrial myopathy showed muscle function improvements (Neurology: DOI: 10.1212/WNL.0000000000005255).

Dosage

10–20 mg/day subcutaneous (SubQ), cycled for 4–8 weeks. Best administered in the morning on an empty stomach to optimize absorption, as food may reduce bioavailability. Consult a healthcare provider for precise timing based on condition.

Side Effects

None significant reported; limited systemic effects in trials, but monitor for potential allergic reactions.

Long-Term Safety

Limited human data; animal studies suggest safety, but ongoing trials are needed for long-term effects. Regular monitoring recommended for extended use.

Legal Status

Research-only, not FDA-approved. Source from compounding pharmacies with a prescription to ensure quality.

CJC-1295

What It Is & How It Works

CJC-1295 is a growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) analog with a drug affinity complex (DAC) for a long half-life, stimulating pituitary growth hormone (GH) release, which increases insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1) levels for sustained anabolic effects.

Benefits

  • Muscle Growth: Enhances protein synthesis, increasing lean muscle mass by boosting GH and IGF-1 levels in muscle tissue.
  • Fat Loss: Increases lipolysis, reducing fat storage, particularly visceral fat, by elevating metabolic rate.
  • Anti-Aging: Boosts GH levels, improving skin elasticity, energy, and vitality, countering age-related decline in tissue function.
  • Recovery: Speeds tissue repair post-exercise or injury by promoting cell regeneration and reducing inflammation.
  • Metabolic Health: Improves insulin sensitivity, aiding glucose metabolism for better metabolic function.

Clinical Studies

Ionescu et al. (2006) demonstrated increased GH and IGF-1 levels in humans (PubMed: 16352683); animal studies support efficacy for muscle and fat metabolism.

Dosage

100–200 mcg/day SubQ, cycled for 8–12 weeks. Best administered in the evening on an empty stomach to align with natural GH secretion peaks, typically 30–60 minutes before bed. Avoid food 2 hours prior to maximize absorption.

Side Effects

Flushing, headache, mild nausea; rare risks include insulin resistance or joint pain with high doses.

Long-Term Safety

Limited long-term human data; short-term studies show safety, but monitor for GH-related risks like insulin resistance or joint issues with prolonged use.

Legal Status

Research-only, commonly sourced from grey market; obtain from reputable suppliers with a prescription for quality assurance.

Benefits
